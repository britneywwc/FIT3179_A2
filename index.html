COFFEE AROUND THE WORLD

THE IMPACTS OF COFFEE

90% of the world's adults consume some sort of caffeine every day, making it the most widely used psychoactive drug on Earth (Pollan 2021).
This is mainly because caffeine heavily impacts an individual's mood and drive for the day.
Coffee is one of the most legally traded agricultural commodities in the world. A total of USD 19 billion of coffee exports were recorded in 2017, which also provided jobs for 125 million people (Voora et al., 2019).

COFFEE ON ECONOMIC GROWTH


As seen in the donut chart, Brazil, Vietnam and Colombia are the top 3 coffee exporting countries in 2018. Coffee export impact on country's economic growth. The total coffee production of each country is in thousand 60 kg bags.

A study done by The Brazil Business states that coffee beans made up 10.2% of Brazil's exported commodities in 2011. This shows that the coffee exporting industry is an essential agribusiness for the people of Brazil and also the rest of the coffee drinkers in the world.
