COFFEE AROUND THE WORLD

THE IMPACTS OF COFFEE

90% of the world's adults consume some sort of caffeine every day, making it the most widely used psychoactive drug on Earth (Pollan 2021).
Coffee is one of the most legally traded agricultural commodities in the world. A total of USD 19 billion of coffee exports were recorded in 2017, which also provided jobs for 125 million people (Voora et al., 2019).

THE ECONOMICS OF COFFEE




As seen in the donut chart, Brazil, Vietnam and Colombia are the top 3 coffee exporting countries in 2018. The total coffee production of each country is in thousand 60 kg bags.
ARABICA 🆚 ROBUSTA
Arabica and Robusta coffee are the most popular type of coffee beans. Brazil is the top coffee producer for Arabica whereas Vietnam tops the list for Robusta production. Brazilian Naturals represent Arabica coffee whereas Robusta represents itself.

Both coffee beans show a similar trend in the fluctuations of coffee prices throughout the years. A significant observation is that Brazilian Naturals (Arabica) coffee is more expensive compared to Robusta.

COFFEE FARMERS

🌱 How are Coffee Farmers supported? 🌱
80% of the world's coffee is produced by smallholders which are people, families who own small parcels of lands. An average coffee farmer only earns a living income which is sufficient to provide food and shelter for their families.

For the wilkinson dot plot, each dot represents $1.5 for every 1kg coffee bean sold
From the chart above, it shows that Dominican Republic coffee farmers are the most paid as of 2013. Farmers in Brazil, Colombia, Guatemala and Uganda are also paid better than other farmers.

COFFEE CONSUMPTION

The isotype dot plot shows how much coffee is consumed for every country. The data is normalised as to show coffee consumption per capita.
