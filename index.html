COFFEE AROUND THE WORLD

THE IMPACTS OF COFFEE

90% of the world's adults consume some sort of caffeine every day, making it the most widely used psychoactive drug on Earth (Pollan 2021).
This is mainly because caffeine heavily impacts an individual's mood and drive for the day.
Coffee is one of the most legally traded agricultural commodities in the world. A total of USD 19 billion of coffee exports were recorded in 2017, which also provided jobs for 125 million people (Voora et al., 2019).

THE ECONOMICS OF COFFEE


As seen in the donut chart, Brazil, Vietnam and Colombia are the top 3 coffee exporting countries in 2018. Coffee export impact on country's economic growth. The total coffee production of each country is in thousand 60 kg bags.

A study done by The Brazil Business states that coffee beans made up 10.2% of Brazil's exported commodities in 2011. This shows that the coffee exporting industry is an essential agribusiness for the people of Brazil and also the rest of the coffee drinkers in the world.
ARABICA 🆚 ROBUSTA
Arabica and Robusta coffee are the most popular type of coffee beans. Brazil is the top coffee producer for Arabica whereas Vietnam tops the list for Robusta production. Brazilian Naturals represent Arabica coffee whereas Robusta represents itself.

Both coffee beans show a similar trend in the fluctuations of coffee prices throughout the years. A significant observation is that Brazilian Naturals (Arabica) coffee is more expensive compared to Robusta.

The price disparity between the two coffee is because Arabica coffee requires more delicate and specific cultivation requirements, such as weather and altitude to grow. Robusta on the other hand is easier to care for due to its adaptability to adverse weather and natural diseases. Arabica coffee tend to be sweeter due to the nature of the tree, whereas Robusta contains the most caffeine out of all coffee types.

COFFEE FARMERS

🌱 How are Coffee Farmers supported? 🌱
80% of the world's coffee is produced by smallholders which are people, families who own small parcels of lands. An average coffee farmer only earns a living income which is sufficient to provide food and shelter for their families. Before a consumer gets their coffee, there is a long coffee supply chain all the way from the farm. Each step along the long process takes away a percentage of the coffee earnings from the farmers.
From the chart above, it shows that Dominican Republic coffee farmers are the most paid as of 2013. Farmers in Brazil, Colombia, Guatemala and Uganda are also paid better than other farmers.

There are ongoing efforts by coffee vendors to combat the wage inequality in coffee farmers. Single Origin coffee is a process directly from farm to consumer where the beans are picked by hand by workers who are paid a fair wage. Due to the nature of this coffee process, the prices of Single Origin coffee tend to be higher compared to normal blended coffee.

So the next time you have the opportunity to try Single Origin coffee, know that your decision will help aid local coffee farmers. Besides, you will get to know all the unique characteristics and specific taste profiles of your coffee.
